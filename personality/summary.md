# Sean — operating profile

Distilled from three assessments that agree on the core type:
- **Personalysis** (Dec 2013, at Eventbrite)
- **Insights Discovery 3.5** (Oct 2014, at FullContact — "Reforming Director, Classic")
- **MBTI Step II Interpretive** (Oct 2016 — INTP)

## Type in one line
INTP with strong Thinking, clear Intuition, clear Introversion, moderate Perceiving — with a Fiery-Red / Cool-Blue presentation. "Reforming Director" archetype.

## MBTI Step I clarity (from 2016 report)
- Introversion: **24 (clear)**
- Intuition: **18 (clear)**
- Thinking: **29 (very clear)**
- Perceiving: **13 (moderate)**

Decision-making style: **Logical and Reasonable.**

## Step II facets (all in-preference unless noted)
- **Ways to connect:** Receiving. **Communicating:** Contained. **Relationships:** Intimate. **Comm style:** Reflective. **Energy:** Quiet.
- **Focus:** Abstract. **Info use:** Imaginative. **Idea use:** Conceptual. **Trust:** Theoretical. **Traditions:** Original.
- **Decision:** Logical. **Actual method:** Reasonable. **Differences:** Questioning. **Disagreements:** Critical. **Execution:** Tough.
- **Organization:** Systematic-Casual *midzone*. **Planning:** Open-Ended. **Time:** Pressure-Prompted. **Routines:** Spontaneous. **Completion:** Emergent.

## Preferred style (consistent across all three)
Logical, analytical, ingenious long-range planner. Dislikes confusion, inefficiency, half-measures, anything aimless. Adept in crisis — will take charge, organize, communicate calm. Once objective is fixed, moves without procrastinating. Natural manager through planning.

More interested in *real things* than abstract theories. Alert to here-and-now. Rigorous standards that take precedence over own and others' personal needs. Independent by nature, but will labor tirelessly for a committed mission. Wants to know what's going on; wants everyone pulling their weight.

## How to communicate with him (synthesis of all three)
**Do:**
- Be thorough, organized, on time.
- Present verifiable facts and evidence.
- Structure your ask — goal, options, trade-offs.
- Appeal to open decision-making; let him question.
- Give him ownership of the conclusion.

**Don't:**
- Tell him what to do or how to do it.
- Leap between topics unstructured.
- "Steal his thunder."
- Come unprepared, disorganized, or ill-equipped.
- Challenge his authority head-on; don't offer unsolicited opinions on personal issues.
- Force quick decisions where others are affected without giving him time to think.

## What motivates him
Status and influence. Autonomy and freedom from supervision. Freedom to be forward-looking. Pioneering opportunities. Relentless drive to competency. Data and verifiable evidence. Stretching goals for self and others.

## What drains or frustrates him
Bureaucratic procedures. Rigid regulations. Methodical, repetitive tasks. Roles that limit creativity or impose conformity. Leadership that is vague, passive, or indirect. Messy, unstructured inputs. Being kept in the dark.

## Blind spots (must actively cover)
- **Appears cold, arrogant, abrupt.** Confidence reads as superiority to others.
- **Puts facts before feelings.** Misses "how will this land."
- **Tells rather than asks.** Can miss possible meanings, implications, connections.
- **Takes on too much, over-extends.** Loses interest when challenge drops.
- **Dismisses non-rational input.** "Takes aspirin for a headache" attitude.
- **Drives others as hard as himself.** Steps on toes to achieve objectives.
- **May not demonstrate appreciation of positive contributions** — natural critic.

## Stress pattern (Personalysis "negative behaviors")
- **First line of defense (Socialized):** Resentful, antagonistic, resists, withholds cooperation. Feels the need to be quiet, exercise restraint, stay with familiar practices.
- **Under deeper fear (Instinctive):** Stubborn, over-controlling, builds a shell to retain order. Can become arbitrary, dictatorial, impulsive.
- **Corrective cues:** Leaders should focus on methodology, be detailed and specific, allow time to sort information. Avoid generalities, unnecessary interruptions, assuming too much.

## Opposite type (Insights)
**Supporter** (Jung's Introverted Feeling). Affable, amiable, loyal, cautious, conventional, diplomatic. Wants appreciation, stability, time, low profile. When working with a Supporter: maintain status quo, give time, respect concern for others' welfare, don't invade space, don't force a quick stance.

## What pal should take from this
1. Default to terse, factual, structured. Options not prescriptions.
2. On every drafted communication for Sean to send, run a tone-check against the Feeling dimension — he won't do it himself.
3. Surface the relational/people cost of any decision he's making. He won't ask.
4. Push back when he's over-committing; cite his own stated priorities.
5. When you detect stress markers (terseness, withdrawal, stubbornness in the chat), slow down, ask what changed, don't push execution.
6. Respect "think aloud" mode — ask questions, don't paste answers.

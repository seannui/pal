# Runbook — meeting prep

When Sean asks "prep me for X" or a calendar event is <2h out with no prep written.

## Inputs
1. Calendar event (who, when, subject, description, attached docs).
2. `people/<attendee>.md` for each attendee (if missing, stub one and flag in `inbox/gaps.md`).
3. Last N email threads with those attendees (Gmail MCP, last 90 days, or since last meeting).
4. `ventures/*.md` and `commitments/open.md` — what's open between Sean and these people.
5. Recent relevant briefs.

## Output — one page, in-chat (no file unless asked)

Template:

```
# Prep — <meeting title> — <time>

## Who
- <name>, <role> — <one-line relationship context>

## Their likely agenda
- <what they want out of this>

## Sean's objective
- <one sentence — what does he want when he walks out?>

## Open threads with them
- <commitment or topic> — <state>

## Three questions to drive the meeting
1. ...
2. ...
3. ...

## One risk / watch-out
<something to not miss — a relational sensitivity, a past friction, a likely misread>

## Recommended next steps to land
- Before meeting: <action>
- After meeting: <action>
```

## Rules
- **Sean's objective must be a single sentence.** If he hasn't defined it, ask before prepping.
- Three questions, not ten. They should be the minimum set to move the meeting forward.
- The watch-out is where Sean's Feeling blind spot gets coverage — surface the relational dimension that he might walk past.
- If the meeting is recurring, read the last meeting's notes/outcomes first. Continuity matters more than freshness.
